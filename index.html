About Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. Earth is our only home. It provides everything we need — clean air, fresh water, food, and a stable climate. Protecting Earth ensures a safe, healthy, and sustainable future for all living beings. It has a rich ecosystem, diverse climates, and breathtaking landscapes that make it unique in our solar system.

Interesting Facts

  • 🌍 Earth is about 4.54 billion years old.
  • 🌊 71% of Earth's surface is covered in water.
  • 🌲 The Amazon Rainforest produces 20% of the world’s oxygen.
  • 🌋 Earth has over 1,500 active volcanoes.

Green Forests

Green forests are vital ecosystems rich in biodiversity. They provide clean air, store carbon, protect wildlife, and regulate the Earth's climate. Forests support livelihoods and offer natural beauty. Their dense canopy, lush vegetation, and abundant life make them essential for environmental balance, water conservation, and combating global warming.

Blue Oceans

Blue oceans cover most of the Earth and are vital for life. They regulate climate, produce oxygen, and support millions of marine species. Oceans provide food, transport, and jobs for people worldwide. Though beautiful and rich in life, they face threats like pollution and overfishing. Protecting them is essential.

Rich Wildlife

Rich wildlife refers to the wide variety of animals and plants found in nature. Forests, oceans, grasslands, and wetlands are home to countless species, each playing a role in the ecosystem. Wildlife helps maintain balance in nature and supports biodiversity. Protecting rich wildlife is important for a healthy planet.
